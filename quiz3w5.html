Week 5: Quiz

All of the questions will be based on the Statement of the Minister of Health on the launch of Mobile Laboratories

       



1. As of this week, how many cases are not allocated provinces in this week?

Answer: 90

2. What other 3 important factors contribute to having too few test cases both in the Public and Private Laboratories and also considering the size of our population?

Answer:Inequality, Poverty and Underlying disease burden

3.The President has approved a “COVID Home Visits Program”, what is this program about?

Answer: The program is about the deployment of Community Health Care Workers to the field for a mass of door-to-door households and community-based screening, referral for clinic testing, quarantine of suspected cases and appropriate care for COVID-19 patients.

4. Who has PEPFAR funded in order to support provinces in this Program that have been availed?

Answer:The District Support Partners

5. Which 2 organizations have assisted the Department of Health in mapping the most vulnerable populations- through this process 993 strategic priority wards were identified using the social vulnerability index?

Answer: The CSIR and Right to Care

6. What is the total number of mobile testing units have been procured and deployed so far, in order to ensure that there is adequate capacity to test citizens being screened?

Answer:67

7. At this stage, the focus will be on collecting what and for what type of test?

Answer:Collecting nasopharyngeal specimens for PCR tests or GeneXpert testing

8. What is the current capacity in the number of tests within 24 hours in 10 laboratories countrywide ?

AnswerThe current capacity is 5000 tests in 24 hours in 10 laboratories countrywide

9. Why at the moment, South Africa has not yet started with using Rapid antibody tests have been used in many countries throughout the world?

Answer This is because authorities are currently working round the clock to assess the efficacy of these tests in our context with the aim to make them available for future application.

10. Why does the health ministry say "what we may currently be experiencing is the calm before a heavy and devastating storm " with reagrds to what is going to happen next month?

Answer:Next month the flu season starts, thus making more people sick with similarsymptoms, and therefore no way of distinguishing common colds from an upsurge of COVID-19 infection. These will flood our clinics and hospitals and create fertile ground for Coronavirus to spread or be masked in its presentation.
